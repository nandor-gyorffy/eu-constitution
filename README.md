# The Constitution of the European Union
### *"In Varietate Concordia" — United in Diversity*

**Published on Europe Day, 9 May 2026**

---

## What is this?

This repository contains a full constitutional draft for a renewed and stronger European Union — written and proposed by Nándor Győrffy, a citizen of Hungary, in the interest of the whole of Europe.

The last serious attempt at EU constitutional reform was the Constitutional Treaty of 2004. It was rejected by French and Dutch voters in 2005, and the project was shelved. Since then, the Lisbon Treaty of 2009 has been the last structural reform of the EU — now seventeen years ago. The world has changed enormously since. The EU's institutional architecture has not kept pace.

This document is a contribution to a debate that should be happening, and largely isn't.

---

## What does it propose?

The Constitution is a complete federal constitutional text — 268 articles across 150 pages. Its core proposals include:

- **A directly elected EU President** — chosen by all EU citizens via universal suffrage every five years, requiring 200,000 candidate recommendations from at least seven different member states
- **A European Congress** — combining the European Parliament and European Council with clear democratic accountability and exclusive power over defence, foreign affairs, currency, and borders
- **A 72-article Bill of Rights** — covering dignity, freedoms, equality, social rights, digital rights, workers' rights, and justice, binding on all member states with no opt-outs
- **Unified European defence** — EU Armed Forces under democratic oversight, financed at a minimum of 3% of EU GDP
- **Rule of law with enforcement** — constitutional requirements for free elections, freedom of speech, and equality before the law in every member state
- **A free and independent European media** — constitutional press freedom protections, media diversity requirements, and the European Broadcasting Corporation operating in all 24 official languages
- **Education as a fifth branch of power** — independent Teachers' Debates governing education, separate from the legislative, executive, judicial, and media branches
- **A shield against autocratic interference** — the European Congress labels autocratic regimes; any party or media organisation receiving their funding faces dissolution
- **Real direct democracy** — 300,000 voters can introduce a bill; 1,000,000 can force a referendum; 500,000 can repeal an existing law
- **A constitutional fiscal framework** — clear principles for taxation, budget rules, and financial equalisation between member states

## Why Europe Day?

On 9 May 1950, Robert Schuman stood up in Paris and proposed something most people assumed was impossible: that France and Germany should pool their coal and steel under a common authority, making war between them materially impossible. His vision was always of something more than a trading bloc — he spoke of a European federation.

That vision was never fully realised. This document is an attempt to continue where he left off.

Seventy-six years later, on the same date, this Constitution is published — not as a finished political programme, but as a contribution to a debate Europe needs to have.
