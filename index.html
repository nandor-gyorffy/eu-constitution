The Moment

Twenty years since the last attempt. The world cannot wait another twenty.

In 2004, Europe tried. A Constitutional Treaty was signed. Then France voted no. The Netherlands voted no. The project died.

Since then: the Lisbon Treaty, Brexit, a pandemic, a war on European soil, the return of great-power competition, and democratic backsliding inside the Union itself.

The EU was designed for a different era. This is a proposal to redesign it — not to erase what exists, but to give it the democratic backbone it has always lacked.

The time for strengthening Europe is now.

Core Proposals

What the Constitution proposes

01 / EXECUTIVE

A Directly Elected EU President

Chosen by all EU citizens via universal suffrage every five years. Two-term limit. A genuine democratic mandate — something no EU leader has ever had.

02 / LEGISLATURE

A European Congress

A reformed bicameral legislature with real accountability. Citizens elect it. It has exclusive power over defence, foreign affairs, currency, and borders.

03 / RIGHTS

A 72-Article Bill of Rights

Dignity, freedom, equality, social rights, digital rights, workers' rights — all constitutionally guaranteed and binding on every member state.

04 / DEFENCE

Unified European Defence

The European Congress maintains EU armed forces. One defence policy. One command. No more 27 different responses to the same threat.

05 / RULE OF LAW

Rule of Law — With Teeth

Every member state must uphold free elections, freedom of speech, and equality before the law. The Constitution provides real enforcement mechanisms.

06 / FISCAL

Common Taxation Framework

A constitutional framework for EU-wide contributions, customs, and budget distribution — ending the annual fights over who pays what.

Context

Why this, why now

The EU's institutional history shows a pattern of reform only in crisis. We are in a crisis. This time, the reform must come before the damage is irreversible.

2004

The Constitutional Treaty

Signed in Rome. Rejected by France and the Netherlands in 2005. The federalist dream shelved.

2009

Lisbon Treaty

A watered-down substitute. Improved some things. Left the democratic deficit intact.

2016

Brexit

The first exit. A warning about the EU's failure to make its case to citizens.

2022

War Returns to Europe

Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine. 27 separate defence policies. 27 separate voices. Europe struggled to speak as one.

2026

This Proposal

A full constitutional draft. Not a treaty amendment. A genuine federal constitution for a Europe that can face the 21st century.
